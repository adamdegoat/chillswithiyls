Your Very First Camping Trip

You don't need to be an outdoors person. You just need a bag, a blanket, and a willingness to sleep somewhere new.

Pack Less Than You Think

The biggest beginner mistake is overpacking. For your first trip, keep it to six things. A tent (borrow one if you can — no need to buy yet). A sleeping bag rated for the temperature you're expecting. A torch or headlamp with fresh batteries. Enough water for the whole trip. Some simple snacks and food. And a warm layer, even in summer, because nights are colder than you expect.

That's genuinely it. Everything else is nice to have, not need to have. You can always upgrade your kit after your first trip, once you know what you actually use.

Pick a Proper Campground, Not the Wilderness

For your very first time, skip the remote bush and book a designated campground. A proper campground has toilets, water, and usually other people nearby. That matters a lot when you're new. You won't have to worry about where to find water or what to do if something goes wrong.

Search for campgrounds with good reviews from families or first-timers. National park campgrounds are often excellent — well-maintained, safe, and genuinely beautiful. Book ahead on weekends, especially in good weather.

Always Arrive in Daylight

This is the one rule that makes everything easier. Arriving before dark means you can see what you're doing when you set up the tent. Putting up a tent for the first time in the dark, with a torch in your mouth, is frustrating. Doing it in the afternoon with good light is actually quite satisfying.

Give yourself at least two hours of daylight after arrival. Explore the site, find the bathrooms, collect some firewood, and get settled before the sun goes down.

Your First Night Might Feel a Little Strange

That's completely normal. The sounds outside are different — branches moving, insects, birds settling. The ground is harder than your bed at home. Your brain is in an unfamiliar place and it notices every little thing.

Most people find that by night two, they sleep better outdoors than they do at home. Give it time. Lie there and listen. It's actually kind of wonderful once you stop fighting it.

The Morning Coffee Tip

Bring a small camping stove or a gas burner, and make yourself a hot drink first thing in the morning. Sit outside with it. Don't look at your phone. Just sit there, hold the warm cup, and look around at where you are.

This is the moment most people fall in love with camping. It doesn't matter if the tent leaked a little, or you woke up at 4am, or your sleeping mat was thin. That quiet morning coffee outside fixes almost everything. It will make you want to come back.
